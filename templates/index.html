The Green Dome

The Green Dome (Arabic: القبة الخضراء, romanized: al-Qubba al-Khaḍrā) is a green-coloured dome built above the tombs of the Islamic prophet Muhammad and the first two caliphs Abu Bakr (r. 632-634) and Umar (r. 634-644). The dome is located in the southeast corner of Al-Masjid al-Nabawi (Mosque of the Prophet) in Medina.

Rawdah ash-Sharifah

The Rawḍah ash-Sharifah (Arabic: روضة الشريفة, lit. 'The Noble Garden') is an area between the minbar and burial chamber of Muhammad. It is regarded as one of the Riyāḍ al-Jannah (Arabic: رِيَاض ٱلْجَنَّة, lit. 'Gardens of Paradise'). A green carpet was used to distinguish the area from the red carpet used in the rest of the mosque, though it is now also green.

Minarets

The first minarets (4 in number) of 26 7.9 m high were constructed by Umar. In 1307, a minaret titled Bab al-Salam was added by Muhammad ibn Kalavun which was renovated by Mehmed IV. After the renovation project of 1994, there were 10 minarets which were 104 metres high. The minarets' upper, bottom and middle portion are cylindrical, octagonal and square shaped respectively.

Door to Enter to Janaza Place

This walled enclosure, on the eastern side of Masjid-e-Nabwi, is the region where the Prophet used to lead Salat-ul-Janaza and the Sahaba continued this tradition.

King Fahad's Entrance

King Fahad gate is named after King Fahad bin Abdul Aziz of Saudi Arabia and is the main entrance to the mosque from the north. Gate no 20, 21, 22 are named King Fahad gate.
